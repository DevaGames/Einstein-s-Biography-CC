Famous Books

1. Relativity: The Special and the General Theory

Author(s): Albert Einstein

This book was made for a general audience. Special Relativity is about the relation between space and time. This introduced the famous e=mc^2 equation. General Relativity explains gravity as the warping of spacetime by mass and energy.

2. The World As I See It

Author(s): Albert Einstein

This book is a collection of Einstein's speeches and essays. It displays his thoughts on topics other than physics. It covers topics such as politics, religion, society, philosophy, and more.

3. Out of my later years

Author(s): Albert Einstein

This book contains a bunch of speechs and essays from the later part of Einstein's life. These cover topics such as science, peace, education, and philosophy.

1. Special Relativity

It introduced the concept that the laws of physics are the same for all non-accelerating observers, and it established the speed of light as a constant in all frames of reference.

Significance: fundamentally changed the understanding of space, time, and energy

2. General Relativity

This theory expanded on special relativity and provided a new description of gravity. Instead of being a force between masses, gravity is understood as the curvature of spacetime caused by mass and energy. Massive objects like stars and planets curve the spacetime around them, and this curvature affects the motion of objects.

Significance: general relativity has been crucial in understanding cosmic phenomena, including black holes, gravitational waves, and the expansion of the universe.

3. Photoelectric Effect

Einstein proposed that light is composed of particles called photons, which carry quantized amounts of energy. This explained how light can eject electrons from a material, a phenomenon that classical wave theories of light could not account for.

Significance: Einstein's explanation of the photoelectric effect provided crucial evidence for the quantum nature of light, helping to establish the field of quantum mechanics
